Why programming?

When I was in high school I took a computer science AP class. This was my first exposure to coding. The whole class was taught in Java. I remember the first time that I ran some code that produced 'Hello World' in the console below. I was ecstatic. Then we started doing cool stuff like loops and conditionals and solving problems with algorithms. I loved the sense of power I got from writing some code that produced some output in front of me. My imagination opened up and I realized that with this knowledge I could create anything I wanted! Despite my interest, I ended up going to Duke for electrical engineering because I looked up to some of my family members that were engineers and I was always ok at math. Duke makes it really easy to double major in electrical engineering and computer science because their electrical engineering major includes computer engineering. I decided to double major and ended up loving the computer science curriculum way more than the electrical engineering curriculum. That's when I decided that I would pursue a career as a software engineer - best of both worlds!
